\begin{abstract}
Hospital equipment management at medical-technology companies such as Philips relies on a complex portfolio of maintenance contracts, manufacturer warranties and warranty extensions. Current decision-support tools remain largely descriptive and provide limited anticipation of contractual risks. We cast the coverage-optimization problem as a finite-horizon Markov decision process (MDP) on a single piece of equipment and train a from-scratch Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) agent in a reproducible simulation environment. Our contribution is not algorithmic: PPO, GAE and observation normalization are taken off-the-shelf. We contribute a clean MDP formulation for a decision-making problem that, to our knowledge, has not been cast as RL in the published literature, together with a statistically rigorous evaluation protocol that includes synchronous value iteration as a tabular Bellman reference. On 100 shared seeds, PPO attains $54.56 \pm 3.93$ (95\% CI $[53.79, 55.33]$), far above random ($-241.44 \pm 92.66$), but below a threshold heuristic ($58.06 \pm 3.93$) and synchronous value iteration ($58.08 \pm 3.96$), which are statistically indistinguishable. The heuristic remains robust across its threshold sweep, and a state-by-state tabulation of the saved PPO policy shows 95.2\% action agreement with VI over 65{,}880 reachable states.
\end{abstract}
